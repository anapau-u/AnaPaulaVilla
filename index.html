FAQ

Formula 1, commonly known as F1, is a high-speed, open-wheel motorsport that involves racing teams and drivers competing in a series of Grand Prix events worldwide. F1 is the pinnacle of motorsport, known for its cutting-edge technology and fast-paced races.

F1 race weekends typically consist of three sessions. On Fridays, followed by qualifying sessions on Saturdays to determine the starting grid for the race. The main event, the Grand Prix race, takes place on Sundays.

DRS (Drag Reduction System) and KERS (Kinetic Energy Recovery System) are technologies used in F1 to enhance racing. DRS allows drivers to reduce aerodynamic drag on straights, promoting overtaking, while KERS recovers and stores energy from braking to provide a temporary power boost.

Teams

Scuderia Ferrari

The iconic Italian racing team, is synonymous with passion, speed, and a rich history in Formula 1. With a legacy spanning decades, Ferrari boasts a record number of Constructors' and Drivers' Championships. Their scarlet red cars and prancing horse logo are instantly recognizable on the F1 grid.

Red Bull Racing

Red Bull Racing, backed by the energy drink giant, has redefined Formula 1 with its youthful energy and daring approach to racing. Known for nurturing talent, Red Bull's dynamic drivers and innovative engineering have made them fierce competitors, challenging the established order of the sport.

McLaren

McLaren, a British racing legend, combines innovation and heritage in Formula 1. With a history of world championships and legendary drivers, McLaren continues to push the boundaries of technology and performance. Their distinctive orange and blue livery is a symbol of excellence on the track.

Mercedes

Mercedes-AMG Petronas Formula One Team is a dominant force in modern Formula 1. With a record-breaking streak of championships, Mercedes' silver arrows showcase cutting-edge technology and unparalleled teamwork.

Haas F1 Team

Haas F1 Team is an American outfit in the world of Formula 1. Known for its distinctive black and white livery, Haas has rapidly climbed the ranks, bringing American flair to the global racing stage.

Williams Racing

Williams Racing boasts a legacy of success in Formula 1. With a focus on engineering excellence, this British team has played a pivotal role in the sport's evolution and remains a respected name on the grid.

Alfa Romeo

Alfa Romeo Racing, with its Italian heritage, blends tradition and modernity in Formula 1. Carrying the iconic Alfa Romeo emblem, the team is a nod to the sport's rich history and continues to compete with style.

Aston Martin

Aston Martin Cognizant Formula One™ Team marries British luxury and racing prowess. With James Bond charm and a commitment to speed, Aston Martin is making a bold return to Formula 1.

Alpine

Alpine F1 Team, formerly Renault, represents French racing excellence. Combining performance with elegance, Alpine brings a touch of French sophistication to the world of motorsport.

Alpha Tauri

Scuderia AlphaTauri embodies the fusion of Italian and Japanese influences. This sister team to Red Bull Racing is known for its dynamic approach and nurturing emerging talents.
